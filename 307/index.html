Bases

Definition: (basis) A basis of VVV is a list of vectors in VVV that is linearly independent and spans VVV.
Theorem: (criterion to be a basis) A list v1,…,vm{ v}^{ }_1,\dots,{ v}^{ }_{ m}v1​,…,vm​ of vectors in VVV is a basis of VVV if and only if every v∈Vv\in Vv∈V can be written uniquely in the form

where a1,…,am∈F{ a}^{ }_1,\dots,{ a}^{ }_{ m}\in\mathbb{ F}a1​,…,am​∈F.

Theorem: (basis of finite-dimensional vector space) Every finite-dimensional vector space has a basis.
Theorem: (every linearly independent list extends to a basis) Every linearly independent list of vectors in a finite-dimensional vector space can be extended to a basis of the vector space.

Dimension

Theorem: (basis length does not depend on basis) Any two bases of a finite-dimensional vector space have the same length.

Definition: (dimension, dim⁡V\dim VdimV)

  • The dimension of a finite-dimensional vector space is the length of any basis of the vector space.

  • The dimension of a finite-dimensional vector space VVV is denoted by dim⁡V\dim VdimV.

Theorem: (linearly independent list of the right length is a basis) Suppose VVV is finite-dimensional, then every linearly independent list of vectors in VVV of length dim⁡V\dim VdimV is a basis of VVV.
Theorem: (subspace of full dimensional equals the whole space) Suppose that VVV is finite-dimensional and UUU is a subspace of VVV such that dim⁡U=dim⁡V\dim U=\dim VdimU=dimV, then U=VU=VU=V.
Theorem: (spanning list of the right length is a basis) Suppose VVV is finite-dimensional, then every spanning list of vectors in VVV of length dim⁡V\dim VdimV is a basis of VVV.
